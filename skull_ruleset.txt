SKULL - SIMPLIFIED RULESET FOR ML TRAINING
==========================================

PLAYERS: 2-6
WIN CONDITION: Successfully complete 2 challenges (or be last player remaining)

COMPONENTS PER PLAYER:
- 4 discs: 3 flowers + 1 skull
- 1 mat (tracks wins: skull-side up = 0 wins, flower-side up = 1 win)

GAME STATE:
- Each player has a hand of discs (starts at 4, decreases on penalties)
- Each player has a mat stack (discs placed face-down, ordered)
- Each player has a win count (0 or 1)
- A current bid (integer, 0 = no bid yet)
- Active players in current round (players who haven't passed)

==========================================
ROUND STRUCTURE
==========================================

PHASE 1 - PLACEMENT
- Each player MUST place exactly 1 disc face-down on their mat.
- Discs are stacked in order of placement (top = last placed).
- Go clockwise starting from the first player.

PHASE 2 - ADD OR BID
- Starting from the first player, each player must do one of:
  A) PLACE: Add 1 more disc face-down on top of their stack.
  B) BID: Announce a number N (1 <= N <= total discs on all mats).
     - This starts the bidding phase. Once bidding starts, no more placing.
  - A player MUST bid (cannot place) if they have no discs left in hand.
  - Minimum bid = 1. Cannot bid 0.

PHASE 3 - BIDDING (after first bid is placed)
- Clockwise from the player after the bidder, each active player must:
  A) RAISE: Announce a number strictly greater than the current bid.
  B) PASS: Slide mat forward; exit this round's bidding permanently.
- Continue until all but one player has passed.
- The last remaining bidder becomes the CHALLENGER with bid = N.

PHASE 4 - CHALLENGE ATTEMPT
- The Challenger must flip exactly N discs total without revealing any skull.
- Rules for flipping:
  1. Challenger MUST flip ALL of their own discs first (top to bottom).
  2. After clearing their own stack, they choose any opponent's mat and
     flip the top disc. Then choose again, and so on.
  3. Discs are always flipped from the TOP of a stack.
  4. Challenger does NOT need to exhaust any one stack before moving to another
     (after flipping the top disc of a stack, they can switch to another).

OUTCOME A - SKULL FLIPPED (failure):
- Challenger immediately stops.
- All players return all their discs to hand.
- Challenger loses 1 disc permanently:
    - Challenger shuffles their remaining discs face-down.
    - The player whose skull was flipped picks 1 disc to remove (without looking).
    - Exception: if Challenger flipped their OWN skull, Challenger picks which disc to lose.
    - Removed disc is permanently out of game (contents unknown to all but Challenger).
- If Challenger now has 0 discs: Challenger is ELIMINATED, returns mat.
    - Next first player = the player whose skull was flipped
      (or Challenger's choice if they flipped their own skull).
- If Challenger still has discs: Challenger becomes first player of next round.

OUTCOME B - N DISCS FLIPPED, NO SKULL (success):
- Challenger flips their mat to flower-side up (if not already).
- If mat was ALREADY flower-side up: Challenger WINS THE GAME immediately.
- Challenger becomes first player of next round.

SPECIAL CASE - 1 DISC REMAINING:
- Player places their only disc in Phase 1 as normal.
- In Phase 2 (adding): they must bid instead of placing.
- In Phase 3 (bidding): they can raise or pass normally.
- In Phase 4 (attempt): if their disc is flipped and it's a skull,
  that player is immediately ELIMINATED.

==========================================
ELIMINATION
==========================================
- A player is eliminated when they lose their last disc.
- Eliminated player removes their mat from the game.
- If only 1 player remains (all others eliminated): that player wins.

==========================================
INFORMATION AVAILABLE TO EACH AGENT
==========================================
PUBLIC (observable by all):
- Number of discs in each player's hand
- Number of discs in each player's mat stack
- Order of face-up (already flipped) discs on each mat
- Number of wins per player (mat orientation)
- Current bid
- Who has passed this round
- Total discs on table

PRIVATE (known only to that player):
- Contents of their own hand
- Contents of their own mat stack (which disc is at which position)

HIDDEN (unknown to all until revealed):
- Contents of opponent mat stacks
- Identity of eliminated discs

==========================================
ACTION SPACE SUMMARY
==========================================
In Phase 1:     PLACE(disc)          -- disc in {flower, skull}
In Phase 2:     PLACE(disc) or BID(n)
In Phase 3:     RAISE(n) or PASS
In Phase 4:     FLIP(player_id)      -- flip top disc of chosen player's stack
                                        (self must be fully cleared first)

==========================================
REWARD SIGNAL SUGGESTIONS
==========================================
- Win game:             +1.0
- Succeed at challenge: +0.3
- Fail challenge:       -0.3
- Lose a disc:          -0.1
- Eliminate opponent:   +0.2
- Be eliminated:        -1.0
